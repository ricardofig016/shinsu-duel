Overview

Shinsu Duel is a 1vs1 collectible card game (CCG) inspired by SIU's Tower of God. The game offers both Player vs. Player (PvP) and Campaign (PvE) modes. Players strategize by building decks, deploying units, and using abilities to destroy their opponent's lighthouses while protecting their own.

Objective

The primary objective is to destroy the opponent's lighthouses. Each lighthouse represents one point of the player's HP. A player loses when they have 0 lighthouses.

Card Types

There are several types of cards in Shinsu Duel, each serving a unique purpose. None of the cards represent how strong a character, weapon, or ability is in the original webtoon, to maintain game balance and encourage diverse strategies.

Unit Cards

Represent characters from Tower of God and are the main focus of the game.

  1. Can be deployed to the battlefield
  2. Have stats like HP and shinsu cost
  3. Fill specific positions
  4. Have traits and affiliations
  5. Can only target backline enemies when the enemy frontline is empty
  6. Can only target the lighthouses when the enemy board is empty
  7. Shinheuh

    Are special units

    1. can only be summoned by animas

Consumables

Items that can be used once to provide a specific effect.

  1. May target specific units, all units, or no units
  2. Playing a consumable ends the turn
  3. e.g.: Lightning Pill, Shinsu Pill, Ignition spells

Equipment

Items that can be attached to allied units to enhance their stats or provide other benefits.

  1. Can be applied to a deployed allied unit
  2. Provide buffs or special abilities
  3. Can't be removed from the unit
  4. Once the unit dies, the equipment returns to hand
  5. Equipping an ally ends the turn
  6. e.g.: Green April, Androssi's Shield, Zahard's Ring

Landmarks

Represent significant locations from Tower of God.

  1. Each player can only have 1 landmark on the battlefield at a time
  2. Provide passive or active effects on the whole battlefield while they are deployed
  3. If the player plays another landmark, the previous one is destroyed and replaced
  4. e.g.: Hidden Floor, Hell Train, The Workshop

Resources

Shinsu

  1. The resource required to play all cards
  2. Players start with 1 shinsu in round 1
  3. Shinsu increases by 1 per round until round 10 (maximum of 10 shinsu per round)
  4. After round 10, Shinsu remains at 10 per round
  5. Up to 2 unspent shinsu can be recharged and used next round
  6. The maximum shinsu a player can have in a round is 12 (10 normal shinsu + 2 recharged shinsu)
  7. Abilities (mainly powerful ones) may also cost shinsu

Lighthouses

  1. Each player starts with 20 lighthouses
  2. Players can gain additional lighthouses with no upper limit
  3. Losing all lighthouses ends the game

Board

The board is divided into different areas for each player.

  1. Hand
  2. Deck
  3. Lighthouses
  4. Shinsu
  5. Battlefield
    1. Frontline: Fisherman, Scout, Wave Controller
    2. Backline: Spear Bearer, Light Bearer

Gameplay

Round Phases

  1. Start of Round
    1. Shinsu recharges
    2. Each player draws one card
  2. Player Turns
    1. Players alternate actions
    2. Each action is a turn
  3. Round End
    1. Both players must pass consecutively to end the round

Actions

Players can perform one of the following actions during their turn:

  1. Play a card
  2. Use an ability
  3. Pass the turn

Card Mechanics

Unit Cards

  1. Stats
    1. Shinsu Cost (blue circle)
    2. HP (heart icon)
  2. Abilities (below the art)
  3. Passive (top-right)
  4. Positions indicate where units can be deployed on the field. Each position generally has a role on the team.
    1. Fisherman: tank damage and deal damage
    2. Light Bearer: provide support in the form of utility, buffs and lighthouses
    3. Scout: provide support in the form of enemy debuffs, damaging protected enemy units and interfere with enemy synergy
    4. Spear Bearer: deal massive amounts of damage
    5. Wave Controller: deal area damage and provide support in the form of shinsu buffs and debuffs
  5. Traits and Affiliations: Additional modifiers and synergies

Consumables, Equipment and Landmarks

  1. Shinsu cost
  2. Effect description

Deck Building Rules

  1. Decks must contain exactly 30 cards
  2. Players may include a maximum of 3 copies of the same card
  3. Starting Hand:
    1. Players draw 4 cards

Vocabulary

List of Keywords

  1. Cleanse: Remove every negative trait
  2. Destroy n: Destroy n enemy lighthouses
  3. Fast: This ability/spell will not end your turn
  4. Silence: Remove every positive trait
  5. Spend n: Spend n shinsu to play this ability

Traits

  1. List of Traits
    1. Positive:
      1. Barrier: Negate all damage the next time I take damage
      2. Bloodthirsty: When I kill a unit, restore 1 HP
      3. Creator: When I am deployed, create 1 lighthouse
      4. Dealer: Draw a card at the start of every round
      5. Immune: Cleanse and I am immune to negative effects
      6. Last One Standing: If I am the only ally unit, I have +4 HP and deal +4 damage
      7. Lethal: When I damage another unit, kill it
      8. Pierce: Destroy 1 enemy lighthouse when I deal damage
      9. Reflect: When I take damage, deal 1 damage to my attacker
      10. Regenerate: Restore me to full HP at the end of each round
      11. Resilient: Take -1 damage from all sources
      12. Ruthless: I deal +1 damage for each lighthouse you have destroyed
      13. Sharpshooter: Can target any enemy
      14. Strong: I deal +1 damage
      15. Taunt: Enemies are forced to target me, if I am targetable
    2. Negative:
      1. Burned: Take 1 damage every turn end, until the end of the round
      2. Cursed: Take 1 damage when I use an ability
      3. Doomed: Die at the end of this round
      4. Exhausted: Deal -1 damage
      5. Ghost: I don't prevent the enemy from targeting what's behind me
      6. Heavy: Abilities cost +1 shinsu
      7. Poisoned: Take 1 damage every round end
      8. Rooted: Can't switch positions
      9. Stunned: Can't use abilities
      10. Weak: Take +1 damage from all sources
  2. Color Coding:
    1. Positive:
      1. Defensive Buffs (Yellow)
      2. Damage Buffs (Orange)
      3. Utility Buffs + HP Gain (Blue)
    2. Negative:
      1. Damage-Dealing Debuffs (Red)
      2. Utility Debuffs (Purple)

Future Features

Team Leader

  1. Certain units may have a Team Leader Effect that activates when they are designated as the leader
  2. Each player can only have one team leader at a time

Evolution

  1. Some units may evolve under specific conditions
  2. Evolution improves a unit's stats and abilities and changes its art (e.g., Khun Ran evolves when given the Lightning Pill)
