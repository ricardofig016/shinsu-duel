Shinsu Duel: A Tower of God CCG

Shinsu Duel is a 1vs1 collectible card game (CCG) inspired by SIU's Tower of God. The game offers both Player vs. Player (PvP) and Campaign (PvE) modes. Players strategize by building decks, deploying units, and using abilities to destroy their opponent's lighthouses while protecting their own.

Overview

Shinsu Duel is a tactical card game where players compete to destroy each other's lighthouses. Players use unit cards to attack, defend, and control the battlefield, while support cards provide additional strategic depth. The game is inspired by the lore, characters, and objects from Tower of God.

Card Types

Unit Cards

Unit cards represent characters from Tower of God (e.g., Zahard, Rak, Urek, Prince).

Key Features:

  1. Can be deployed to the battlefield
  2. Have stats like HP and shinsu cost
  3. Fill specific positions
  4. Have traits and affiliations
  5. Can only target backline enemies when the enemy frontline is empty
  6. Can only target the lighthouses when the enemy board is empty

Support Cards

Represent objects, spells, effects, and other elements from the webtoon.

Subtypes:

  1. Equipment (e.g., Green April, Androssi's Shield, Zahard's Ring)
  2. Spells (e.g., Ignition spells)
  3. Consumables (e.g., Lightning Pill)

Typically used to enhance or evolve units, manipulate the battlefield, or provide other strategic advantages.

Objective

The primary objective is to destroy the opponent's lighthouses. Each lighthouse represents one point of the player's nexus HP. A player loses when they have 0 lighthouses.

Resources

Shinsu

  1. The resource required to play cards
  2. Recharge Rules:
    1. Players start with 1 shinsu in round 1
    2. Shinsu increases by 1 per round until round 10 (maximum of 10 shinsu per round)
    3. Up to 2 unspent shinsu can be recharged and used next round
    4. Maximum Shinsu Cap: 12 (10 round shinsu + 2 recharged shinsu)

Lighthouses

  1. Each player starts with 20 lighthouses
  2. Players can gain additional lighthouses with no upper limit
  3. Losing all lighthouses ends the game

Board Layout

The board is divided into:

  1. Player Side:
    1. Hand (cards currently held)
    2. Deck (remaining cards)
    3. Lighthouse area (indicating nexus HP)
    4. Shinsu counter (current shinsu)
  2. Battlefield:
    1. Divided into Frontline and Backline for each player
      1. Frontline: Fisherman, Scout, Wave Controller
      2. Backline: Spear Bearer, Light Bearer

Gameplay

Turn Phases

  1. Start of Round:
    1. Shinsu recharges
    2. Each player draws one card
  2. Player Turns:
    1. Players alternate actions
    2. Actions include playing cards, using abilities, or repositioning units
  3. Round End:
    1. Both players must pass consecutively to end the round

Actions and Free Actions

  1. Actions:
    1. Play a card
    2. Use an ability
    3. Change a unit's position
    4. Pass the turn
  2. Free Actions (do not end the turn):
    1. Remove equipment from a unit
    2. Play a "fast" support card
    3. Use a "fast" ability

Round End and Priority Token

  1. The player with the priority token starts the round
  2. At the end of a round, the priority token passes to the other player

Card Mechanics

Unit Cards Mechanics

  1. Stats:
    1. Shinsu Cost (top-left circle)
    2. HP (heart icon)
  2. Abilities:
    1. Active abilities
    2. Passive effects (always active)
  3. Positions indicate where units can be deployed on the field. Each position generally has a role on the team:
    1. Fisherman: tank damage and deal damage
    2. Light Bearer: provide support in the form of utility, buffs and lighthouses
    3. Scout: provide support in the form of debuffs, damaging protected enemy units and interfere with enemy synergy
    4. Spear Bearer: deal massive amounts of damage
    5. Wave Controller: deal area damage and provide shinsu related support and shinsu related debuffs
  4. Traits and Affiliations: Additional modifiers and synergies

Support Cards Mechanics

  1. Simpler structure compared to unit cards
  2. Key Features:
    1. Background and rarity border
    2. Shinsu cost
    3. Effects vary based on subtype

Deck Building Rules

  1. Decks must contain exactly 20 cards
  2. Players may include a maximum of 3 copies of the same card
  3. Starting Hand:
    1. Players draw 5 cards and may mulligan any number of them

Vocabulary

List of Keywords

  1. Cleanse: Remove every negative trait
  2. Destroy n: Remove n enemy lighthouses
  3. Fast: You can take another action after using this ability/spell
  4. Silence: Remove every positive trait
  5. Spend n: Spend n shinsu to play this ability

Traits

  1. List of Traits
    1. Positive:
      1. Barrier: Negate all damage the next time I take damage
      2. Bloodthirsty: When I kill a unit, restore 1 HP
      3. Creator: When I am deployed, create 1 lighthouse
      4. Dealer: Draw a card at the start of every round
      5. Immune: Cleanse and I am immune to negative effects
      6. Last One Standing: If I am the only ally unit, I have +4 HP and deal +4 damage
      7. Lethal: When I damage another unit, kill it
      8. Pierce: Destroy 1 enemy lighthouse when I deal damage
      9. Reflect: When I take damage, deal 1 damage to my attacker
      10. Regenerate: Restore me to full HP at the end of each round
      11. Resilient: Take -1 damage from all sources
      12. Ruthless: I deal +1 damage for each lighthouse you have destroyed
      13. Sharpshooter: Can target any enemy
      14. Strong: I deal +1 damage
      15. Taunt: Enemies are forced to target me, if I am targetable
    2. Negative:
      1. Burned: I take 1 damage every round end
      2. Cursed: I take 1 damage when I use an ability
      3. Doomed: Die at the end of this round
      4. Exhausted: I deal -1 damage
      5. Ghost: Don't prevent the enemy from targeting what's behind me
      6. Heavy: Abilities that cost shinsu cost +1 shinsu
      7. Poisoned: Every turn end, take 1 damage
      8. Rooted: Can't switch positions
      9. Stunned: Can't use abilities
      10. Weak: Take +1 damage from all sources
  2. Color Coding:
    1. Positive:
      1. Defensive Buffs (Yellow)
      2. Damage Buffs (Orange)
      3. Utility Buffs + HP Gain (Blue)
    2. Negative:
      1. Damage-Dealing Debuffs (Red)
      2. Utility Debuffs (Purple)

Future Features

Team Leader

  1. Certain units may have a Team Leader Effect that activates when they are designated as the leader
  2. Each player can only have one team leader at a time

Evolution

  1. Some units may evolve under specific conditions
  2. Evolution improves a unit's stats and abilities (e.g., Khun Ran evolves when given the Lightning Pill)
